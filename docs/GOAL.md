# Goal & Vision

## What We're Trying to Achieve

Showcase [emergencyAPI.com](https://emergencyapi.com) as a valuable supplementary data source for Australian power utilities — specifically demonstrating how real-time emergency incident data can be integrated into a utility's existing operational platforms to provide **pre-warning** of threats near infrastructure.

## The Core Problem We're Illustrating

Power utilities currently operate reactively:
- An outage occurs → they find out → they respond
- A bushfire approaches infrastructure → they may not know until it's too late
- A storm rolls through → they're reacting to damage, not anticipating it

emergencyAPI.com provides a **pre-warning layer** — live incident data from every Australian state and territory emergency service, available via a single clean API.

## The Value Proposition

> "Your outage management system tells you what's already broken. emergencyAPI.com tells you what's about to break."

With the emergencyAPI.com feed integrated:
- A CFS bushfire alert 2km from a zone substation triggers before the outage
- A storm system approaching a transmission corridor gives advance warning
- An accident blocking a road to a remote substation alerts before crews are dispatched

## Target Audience

**Primary:** South Australian power utility stakeholders (operations managers, network engineers, emergency management coordinators, CTOs)

**Secondary:** Power utilities in all other Australian states — NSW, VIC, QLD, WA, TAS, NT, ACT. The SA showcase translates directly to any state since emergencyAPI.com covers the whole country.

**Tertiary:** Any Australian infrastructure operator who could benefit from real-time emergency proximity awareness (water utilities, gas networks, transport authorities).

## Why South Australia

- emergencyAPI.com provides the richest data coverage for SA (6 agency feeds: CFS, SES, MFS, CFS-PAGER, SES-PAGER, BOMBER-PAGER)
- The project owner is an ex-SA Power Networks employee with deep familiarity of SA infrastructure, geography, and utility operations
- Owner has an existing professional network within SAPN
- SA has diverse and interesting emergency contexts — Adelaide Hills bushfires, Eyre Peninsula weather events, Riverland floods

## Distribution Strategy

**YouTube video** — screen-recorded walkthrough of the live dashboard, uploaded with optimised tags for discoverability:
- Target search terms: "Australian emergency data API", "bushfire infrastructure monitoring", "power utility incident management", "real-time emergency alerts utility", "emergencyAPI Australia"

**Why YouTube:**
- Permanently discoverable via search
- Reaches utility engineers, operations staff, and technical leads organically
- Video format demonstrates the live/real-time nature of the data better than any static page
- Shareable link for direct outreach to SAPN contacts and broader network

## Success Criteria

1. The dashboard looks credible — a utility engineer watches and immediately thinks "that's what our control room could look like"
2. emergencyAPI.com is clearly the star — every data point traces back to the API
3. The data is visibly real — live incidents, real substation locations, authentic SA geography
4. A utility technical lead watching thinks "I could integrate that in a sprint"
5. The video ranks in YouTube search results for relevant utility/emergency data terms

## What This Is NOT

- A production application for utilities to use
- A full outage management system
- A replacement for SCADA or existing utility platforms
- SA Power Networks branded software
- A paid product (emergencyAPI.com has a free tier — that's the entry point)
